I'm not your typical UI/UX Developer...

My original plan was to work in sales with a YouTube network and change our culture through entertainment programming. But my desire to help the peers on my team as much as I can, helped me find my true passions and strengths. After General Assembly, I worked a contract job as a UX Designer. Every day involved selling ideas or solving problems through design. Suprisingly my broad background prepared me to work as a web designer.

My experience working as a barber for 10 years translated in to the same thought process with my work in UX and UI design by having an eye for small details. During college I also DJ'd parties and that helped me understand the importance of great timing. The same applies in web animations to delight users. Working in social media helped me understand the importance of testing. We don't need to have all the answers by the time we launch a campaign. But we should learn and improve what we do day after day.

I can't compete with the years of experience other UI/UX Developers and Designers may have, but I have the technical knowledge, the vision and ability to create. I also have some great mentors around me who help me understand where I could improve. Last, but not least I have the empathy to not only help users, but my team and my community.

If I can't do something now, I'll learn it and at some point, I'll become great at it. My drive to continuously grow has helped me get to this point. But there's no point in winning if I can't help those around me develop in to who they want to be. Crafting digital experience is what I love and its how I'd like to leave my mark.
