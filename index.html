I know I'm not your typical UI/UX Developer...

My original plan was to work in sales with a YouTube network and have a say in changing our culture through entertainment. But my desire to want to help the peers on my team as much as I can, helped me find my true passion and strengths.

After General Assembly, I worked a contract gig as a UX Designer and I was able to find a path where I completely threw all of my self in to my work. Every day involved selling ideas or solving problems through design. My zest to learn the UX Design craft continued day after day.

I was a barber for 10 years as a young kid trying to make some extra money. I never knew that experience would translate into the same kind of thinking with UX or UI design, but it did. During college I also DJ'd parties and that experience helped me understand the importance of great timing. The same applies in web animations to delight users.

In my opinion great UI is what shapes great UX. I applaud you if your site works. There's a lot of work that goes into something that looks simple to create for end users. But is it easy enough to understand for users? Is it intuitive? And at the same time, does it accomplish what you want it to?

I can't compete with the years of experience other UI/UX Developers and Designers may have, but I have the technical knowledge, the vision and ability to create as well as some of the best mentors who help me understand where I could improve. Last, but not least I have the empathy to want to help users, my team and my community.

If I can't do something now, I'll learn it and at some point, I'll become great at it. My drive to help my team's growth is what drives me. There's no point in winning if I can't help those around me grow. Crafting digital experience is what I love and its how I'd like to leave my mark.
