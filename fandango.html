Redesign of FandangoNOW

Watching the latest movies and TV shows in the comfort of your own home has never been easier. And as the window continues to close sooner for films moving from theaters to at home entertainment, FandangoNOW is in a great position to become an industry leader by offering consumers opportunities to buy and rent the newest movies at home or on the go. With continued year over year growth, the goal of the redesign was to form a unified design system across all of our platforms.

My role was to lead the UI redesign of our Android mobile app while working between development, project and product teams.

Created with Material Design

Our latest release that went out in April of 2018 utilized Google's material design language to guide interactions, colors and reusable components. The release also incorporated a few new features that included a discover page that showcased our newest content and a more modern media details page for movie and TV content.

The Big Picture

One of our biggest challenges this year was to make our different products such as, Fandango and FandangoNOW, align cohesively as brands. Re-designing the Android app was one part of an entire ecosystem since we feature our app on iOS, Android, Android TV, Samsung and a variety of other devices.

A Huge Step in the Right Direction

Our previous re-design release went out in 2015 which doesn't seem that long ago. But after multiple iterations and user tests its proven to be a major improvement for current and new users. Of course no re-design goes without flack, so hopefully as we continue to listen to consumer feedback our experiences will only get better with time.

Experimental Interaction Concepts

As a team we recognize that a lot of our customer interactions are very transactional. So creating moments where we put a smile on someone's face or add some element of surprise and delight will be a major focus for us this year. I'm far from a motion graphics designer, but I created these concepts to spark some ideas.
